About this Graph

The stacked area chart is similar to the area chart in that it is defined by a line and the area below it is colored. The difference is that in the stacked area chart, several areas are shown. They are usually depicted in different colors, and the area is representative of the value. This kind of graph is useful for cummulative sets of data. For example, a good use of this kind of graph would be to have the amount of hours spent each week for development, debug, and testing. This would show if more time has been spent on debugs over time as compared to development, showing that perhaps something may need to change. This kind of graph may not be as effective if dealing with many different categories as it may become more difficult to tell the small differences between areas.

Marks and Channels

Marks

Marks include area

Channels

Channels include the size of the area as well as the color of the area, which is used to denote the category.
