About this Graph

Maps are similar to the choropleth in that they show information based on geographical location. Maps can have a variety of overlays, including points for case information, zooming and panning capabilities. If one needs to show complex information which benefits from geographical representation, using a map with these different layers may be a good way to prevent any information from being lost. A great example is Google Maps. Different information is shown when zooming in or out.

Marks and Channels

Marks

Marks include area or containment of the geographical information, as well as potentially points or lines as links for other information placed as overlays to the underlying choropleth.

Channels

Channels include color which is used to categorize different elements, as well as spatial region, placement on a common scale, area, length, or others depending on the type of information being displayed for the overlays.
